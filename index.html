About Me

My name's Sina Khoshnaw. I’m a software engineering student at Marcy Lab School, currently building my foundation in full-stack web development. I grew up in Kurdistan, and one thing I love about where I’m from is the hospitality shown by people and treating each other with respect. Before joining Marcy Lab School, I was exploring different career paths and teaching myself technology on my own. I became interested in software engineering because I enjoy building websites that actually help people. Getting here wasn’t easy, but overcoming challenges taught me persistence, discipline, and how to keep going even when things feel difficult.

Technology inspires me because it allows me to solve real problems and create things people can actually use. I like the idea that something I build can make someone’s life easier or more efficient. What I enjoy most about programming is problem-solving. I like testing ideas, and figuring out why something doesn’t work and how to fix it.

Technical skills I’m developing: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

Non-technical skills I bring: Communication, Problem-solving, Teamwork, Organization

Beyond Coding: Outside of coding, I enjoy learning new skills, staying active, and working on personal growth. I care about self-improvement and building a stable future through technology.
